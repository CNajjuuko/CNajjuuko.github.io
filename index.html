01
Monitoring HIV Treatment Adherence Using Machine Learning
Published In Progress
▼
Machine Learning-Based Risk Prediction for ART Adherence Published · AIDS

Developed and evaluated predictive models to identify adolescents at high risk of suboptimal antiretroviral therapy (ART) adherence in low-resource settings. Applied ensemble methods — Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, and XGBoost — to socio-behavioral and psychosocial data from 702 adolescents living with HIV in Uganda, achieving AUC of 0.71. Models identified key predictors including depression symptoms, stigma experiences, and caregiver support to guide targeted interventions in resource-limited HIV care programs.

View paper →
AI-Based Adherence Monitoring Using Clinical LLMs In Progress

Developing a large language model-based system for automated adherence monitoring using routinely collected health record data in HIV clinics. Implementing NLP and LLM pipelines to extract adherence indicators from clinical notes and detect ART non-adherence patterns early, enabling timely clinical intervention in resource-limited settings.

02
Machine Learning for Substance Use and HIV Risk Monitoring
Published In Progress
▼
Predictors of Illicit Substance Use in Young Adults with HIV Published · AIDS and Behavior

Applied machine learning to longitudinal cohort data from 702 young adults living with HIV in low-resource Ugandan communities to identify key predictors of problematic substance use. Used feature importance analysis and SHAP values to interpret model predictions, surfacing behavioral, psychosocial, and structural risk factors including depression, household instability, and peer influence. Findings inform targeted screening and prevention strategies within HIV care programs.

View paper →
UMAT-R AI: Multimodal Prediction of 30-Day Substance Use and HIV-Risk Outcomes In Progress

Designing an AI pipeline that integrates baseline surveys, app engagement metrics, and coach-client text messages from the UMAT-R digital intervention to enable early risk prediction and support personalized recovery and HIV-prevention care. Developing text-only and multimodal predictive models using TF-IDF/Word2Vec and clinical BERT variants (ClinicalBERT, BioClinicalBERT), combined with survey features. Ongoing work includes transformer-based topic modeling (BERTopic) and self-supervised adaptation of transformer encoders on UMAT-R messages.

03
Hybrid AI Systems for Infection Surveillance and Clinical Workflows
Accepted · Clinical Infectious Diseases
▼
Hybrid LLM + Rules-Based CAUTI Surveillance Accepted

Developed and validated a hybrid surveillance system combining rule-based NHSN logic with LLM-driven symptom extraction from clinical notes for catheter-associated urinary tract infection (CAUTI) surveillance. Implemented the Clinical Entity Augmented Retrieval (CLEAR) framework using BIOMed_NER for entity recognition, BioClinicalBERT embeddings for semantic similarity, and GPT-4o for symptom extraction from flagged cases at Barnes-Jewish Hospital (2021–2024). The hybrid system achieved 90.0% sensitivity and 93.5% specificity — outperforming rules-based approaches (80.4% sensitivity) and standalone LLM methods (88.7% sensitivity) — while reducing infection preventionist chart review workload by approximately 66%. Co-first author.

04
Quantifying Uncertainty in Clinical Prediction Models
In Progress
▼
Conformal Prediction for Emergency Medical Services In Progress

Building and evaluating conformal prediction-based models for emergency medical services (EMS) that predict intervention protocols, medication types, and quantities from multimodal data combining free-text symptom descriptions and vital signs. Developing calibrated, class-fair prediction sets and selective-classification strategies to provide trustworthy decision support for high-stakes clinical interventions.

Uncertainty Quantification Methods for Clinical Language Models In Progress

Conducting a benchmark study characterizing uncertainty quantification methods for clinical language models, evaluating their utility and reliability in real-world clinical prediction settings. Comparing approaches including Monte Carlo dropout, ensemble methods, and calibration techniques across multiple clinical tasks.

05
Maternal and Child Health Service Utilization in Sub-Saharan Africa
Published · Nature Communications
▼
Patterns of MCH Services Utilization and Socioeconomic Disparities Published · Nature Communications

Analyzed patterns of maternal and child health services utilization and associated socioeconomic disparities across sub-Saharan Africa using machine learning on Demographic and Health Survey data from 352,363 mother-child pairs across 35 countries. Identified critical service utilization patterns linked to child mortality outcomes, providing evidence for targeted policy and health system strengthening.
